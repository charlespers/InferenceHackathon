## 0. Executive Summary

At batch=1, Qwen3-235B-A22B decode on 8xH100 is purely HBM-bandwidth bound: TPOT = per-GPU bytes-moved-per-token / usable HBM BW, plus non-overlapped latency (comms sync, kernel launch, sampling, host overhead). Arithmetic intensity is ~1 (GEMV), so the tensor cores are ~99% idle and FLOPs are irrelevant — the entire optimization is "move fewer bytes, expose less latency, and amortize the fixed per-step weight read over more accepted tokens." The single most load-bearing correction to the existing playbook is that EP8, the standard MoE-serving win, is the WRONG axis at B=1: with one token, its 8 experts cannot statistically balance across 8 ranks (balls-in-bins E[max]=2.6), so decode waits on the busiest rank every step — an honest re-floor of TP2xEP8 to ~285 tok/s at short context (not the optimistic ~470-630). Pure TP8 (experts column-sharded) structurally eliminates that routing-imbalance tax and folds the expert reduction into the existing attention all-reduce (fewer collectives, not more), reaching ~547 tok/s short-context. The verified config also corrects the byte budget: NO shared expert exists, all 94 layers are MoE, attention is 6.70B active (64 Q heads x 128 head_dim = 8192 inner dim, not 4096), routed experts are 14.19B (66%), total active 21.57B. The ranked roadmap is led by config-level wins (CUDA graphs, contiguous KV, single-shot prefill, prefix caching, FP8) then the structural TP8 re-layout and FP8->FP4-mixed weights, then speculative decoding (EAGLE-3/MTP, the one orthogonal multiplier that beats the per-step weight floor, realistic tau 1.74-2.2 on this MoE with a c~0.27 expert-union verify tax), then long-context-gated KV work (KIVI-INT4, crossover ~64-89K), then research-grade megakernel/persistent-kernel and custom GEMV work. Honest stacked estimate: from a naive ungraphed FP8 EP8 baseline (~tens of tok/s) to roughly 600-950 tok/s end-to-end at ctx<=8K (FP8 TP8 + spec), or ~1000-1500 with FP4-mixed weights — against a weight-only roofline ceiling near 994 tok/s (TP8 FP8) / ~1900 (FP4). These are NOT a naive product of per-lever multipliers; most levers overlap on the same weight term and share a fixed comms/sampling floor.

**The headline ceilings** (single-stream, ctx≤8K):

| Config | Weight-only roofline | Realistic end-to-end |
|---|---|---|
| FP8, TP2×EP8 (playbook) | ~630 tok/s | ~470–530 tok/s |
| FP8, **pure TP8** | ~994 tok/s | ~500–547 tok/s |
| FP4-mixed weights, TP8 | ~1500–1700 tok/s | ~1000–1500 tok/s |
| + speculative decoding | (orthogonal ×) | adds on top of the above |

**Honest cumulative estimate (NOT a product of multipliers):**

> Do NOT multiply the per-lever multipliers — most overlap on the same weight-read term and share a fixed ~0.3-0.5ms comms/sampling/host floor. Honest cumulative picture at ctx<=8K, FP8: (a) The roofline CEILING is set by per-GPU bytes / 2.68 TB/s. Pure TP8 FP8 weight-only = 1.04 GB/GPU / 2.68 TB/s = ~994 tok/s; after the ~0.28ms 8-way all-reduce floor + sampling, realistic TP8 FP8 e2e ~= 500-547 tok/s. (b) Starting point: a naive ungraphed FP8 EP8 stack realizes only tens of tok/s (launch tax + EP imbalance + host overhead). (c) Stacking the roadmap WITHOUT double-counting: graphs+fast-path+contiguous-KV get you to the GPU-bound regime; the structural EP8->TP8 re-layout is the big honest jump (~285 -> ~547 tok/s, it RE-ALLOCATES where bytes live rather than removing them); spec-decode is the only clean multiplicative lever on top (~x1.2 short-ctx) -> ~600-700 tok/s e2e at ctx<=8K on FP8. (d) Adding FP4-mixed weights lowers the weight floor (~994 -> ~1500-1900 weight-only ceiling) and the SAME spec multiplier rides it -> realistic ~1000-1500 tok/s e2e. (e) Long-context (64-128K) is gated by the nkv=4 KV wall: TP cannot split KV beyond 4 heads, so KV-read co-dominates and the achievable rate falls to ~250-450 tok/s even with KIVI-INT4 + spec. Bottom line: a defensible 8xH100 single-stream target is ~600-950 tok/s (FP8) / ~1000-1500 tok/s (FP4-mixed, accuracy-gated) at short context, i.e. roughly 50-75% of the moving roofline ceiling after the irreducible comms+sampling floor. The remaining gap to ceiling is the latency-bound comms floor and EP/TP all-reduce serialization, which only a megakernel with in-kernel NVSHMEM can fully close.

## Quick reference — genuinely non-obvious findings

1. EP is the WRONG axis at B=1: balls-in-bins E[max]=2.6 experts on the busiest of 8 ranks gates every decode step, so the standard TP2xEP8 is honestly ~285 tok/s (not 470-630); pure TP8 with column-sharded experts structurally eliminates the imbalance tax AND has fewer collectives (the expert reduction folds into the existing attention all-reduce, adding no all-to-all). This inverts the playbook's #1 recommendation.
2. The verified config kills two roofline phantoms: Qwen3-235B-A22B has NO shared expert and NO dense layers (all 94 are top-8 MoE), and attention is 6.70B active not ~1B (64 Q heads x 128 head_dim = 8192 inner dim). The real dominant TP-shardable non-expert mass is 7.4B, which is exactly why TP8 (not EP) minimizes per-GPU bytes; and because you read only the 8 ACTIVE experts (~0.89GB total) per token, EP's '/8 on the 14.2B expert mass' is illusory at B=1 — sharding inactive experts saves zero bandwidth, only HBM capacity (which fits).
3. At B=1 on an MoE, the wall-clock metric is mean accepted LENGTH tau (a draft-tree PATH statistic), not the per-token acceptance RATE the console currently shows — and the MoE expert-union verify tax (c~0.27/draft-token, ~5x a dense model's, because tree positions route to different top-8 sets) bends the speedup curve, making dense bushy-tree defaults net-negative. Prescribe narrow/deep trees and surface tau live.
4. Quantize the BIG thing HARDEST: the 66% of bandwidth in routed experts is also the LEAST quant-sensitive (averaging over 8 + fine-grained 1536 FFN), while the discrete router (0.3% of bytes) is the MOST sensitive and should never be quantized — the opposite of the usual 'protect the big GEMMs' intuition. And every 'FP4 only helps on Blackwell' caveat is a THROUGHPUT statement: at B=1 the Hopper dequant-to-FP8 path captures the full bandwidth (latency) win because compute has slack.
5. Standard serving machinery is anti-B=1: paged KV, continuous-batch scheduler, chunked prefill, PD-disaggregation, W4A4, NVLS/SHARP, LL128, max-channels=32 are all THROUGHPUT advice that is neutral-to-harmful at B=1. vLLM's own profiling shows 62% of a low-batch step is CPU-side (33% API-server, 29% scheduling); the right move is to turn this machinery OFF, not tune it. The MoE all-to-all is 8KB (~18ns of wire time vs ~2us fixed latency) so it is 100% a latency event — bandwidth comms tuning optimizes the wrong axis.
6. The CPU-offload expert-prefetch literature (EdgeMoE/AdapMoE/Fiddler/SP-MoE) is MOOT here: all 235GB FP8 weights are HBM-resident, so there is no PCIe fetch to hide. The only legitimate 'prefetch' would be a deterministic shared expert — which this model doesn't have. L2-pinning the shared expert is physically impossible (50MB L2 vs 300-500MB); only the tiny router/scale/norm tensors pin.
7. SRAM machines (Cerebras/Groq) invert on MoE: sparsity (22B/235B active) is a GIFT to HBM machines (read only active) but a CURSE to SRAM machines (must STORE all 235B on-chip -> ~6 WSE-3 wafers or ~1000 LPUs), and dynamic top-8 routing breaks their static dataflow scheduling. Their dense single-stream records do not transfer to this MoE.

## 1. The Physics Frame and the Roofline Ceiling

### 1.1 The one equation

At batch size 1, decode is a sequence of **GEMV**s (matrix x vector), arithmetic intensity ~1 FLOP/byte — three orders of magnitude below the H100's ~590 FLOP/byte FP8 ridge. The tensor cores are ~99% idle. FLOPs are not the constraint; **bytes-moved-per-token and exposed latency are**. The governing equation:

```
TPOT = bytes_moved_per_token / usable_HBM_BW
       + non-overlapped latency (comms sync + kernel launch + sampling + host)
```

Each token re-reads (a) the *active* weights once and (b) the *entire* KV cache once. There is no batch dimension to amortize the weight read over — that is the entire reason B=1 is special and why most "LLM serving" advice (which is throughput advice) is neutral-to-harmful here.

### 1.2 The verified byte budget (from config.json, not estimates)

Confirmed against `Qwen/Qwen3-235B-A22B/config.json`: hidden=4096, **94 layers, all MoE** (decoder_sparse_step=1, mlp_only_layers empty), **128 experts top-8, NO shared expert**, moe_intermediate=1536, **64 Q heads / 4 KV heads, head_dim=128** (so Q/O proj inner dim = 8192, not 4096), vocab=151936, untied embeddings. The exact arithmetic (each value recomputed below in §1.6) gives Total active = **21.57B**, and full model 235.09B (checks out exactly).

| Component | Active params/token | Share | Quant sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Routed experts (top-8) | 14.19 B | 66% | **LOW** (averaging over 8 + fine 1536 FFN) |
| Attention (Wq/Wk/Wv/Wo) | 6.70 B | 31% | MEDIUM-HIGH (RoPE, outliers) |
| Router (gate) | 0.049 B | 0.2% | **EXTREME** (discrete top-8 selection) |
| lm_head (untied) | 0.62 B | 3% | HIGH (argmax flips) |
| **Total active** | **21.57 B** | | |

**This kills two roofline-frame phantoms.** (1) There is no shared expert and no dense layer — the frame's "~3B shared/dense" term does not exist; FFN is 100% top-8 routed. (2) Attention is **6.70B, not ~1B** — 64 heads x 128 head_dim = 8192 inner dim. Attention is the #2 byte consumer at 31%, and it is TP-shardable but NOT EP-shardable, which (Section 4) flips the optimal layout.

### 1.3 Weight-read floor by precision

Active bytes / 2.68 TB/s usable (= 0.80 x 3.35 TB/s — **see §1.7 for a sensitivity sweep on this 0.80 constant, which moves the whole ceiling +/-10%**):

| Precision | Active bytes | tok/s (8-GPU aggregate TP8, weight-only) |
|---|---|---|
| FP16 | 43.1 GB | 470GB total >> capacity gracefully but no BW benefit over FP8 — irrelevant |
| FP8 | 21.6 GB | **~994** (2.695 GB/GPU / 2.68 TB/s) |
| FP4 (Hopper: dequant-to-FP8, BW-only) | 10.8 GB | **~1900** (with realized-BW haircut, §1.8, ~1500-1700 realistic) |

### 1.4 KV read and the nkv=4 wall

KV per cached position = 2(K,V) x 4 KV heads x 128 x 94 layers = **96,256 elements** = 188 KB FP16 / 94 KB FP8 / 48 KB INT4. This model is already at the aggressive end of GQA (16:1), so the KV term is small in absolute bytes and the crossover where KV-read overtakes weight-read is LATE.

Critically, **nkv=4 caps KV-head TP-splitting at 4 ways.** Beyond TP4 you must replicate KV, re-inflating the per-GPU KV read. **This interacts directly with the recommended weight-TP8 layout and is the single most-confused long-context number in prior drafts — fully reconciled in §1.9.**

### 1.5 The roofline ceiling (busiest-rank ledgers shown explicitly)

The flagship claim of this document — *EP8 is the wrong axis* — rests on a busiest-rank byte ledger that MUST be shown, not asserted. Prior drafts quoted "3.82 GB" and "~285 tok/s" in the same row; **neither is correct and they are mutually inconsistent** (3.82 GB / 2.68 TB/s = 701 tok/s, not 285). Here is the corrected ledger. Per-GPU usable BW = 2.68 TB/s; tok/s = 2680 GB·s⁻¹ / busiest-GPU bytes.

**Key correction the critic demanded: imbalance multiplies ONLY the expert term, not the whole read.** Attention (6.70 B = 31%) is balanced under *any* layout (it is either replicated or evenly TP-sharded — routing never touches it). The balls-in-bins tax applies solely to the 66% expert mass.

One active expert FP8 = 14.19 GB / 8 = **1.774 GB/GPU**. Balls-in-bins (8 balls, 8 bins) gives **E[max on busiest rank] = 2.60 experts** (verified by enumeration). So busiest-rank expert bytes = 2.60 x 1.774 = **4.61 GB**.

| EP8 sub-variant | Attention term | + Busiest experts | + router+lm | **Busiest-GPU total** | tok/s |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **EP8, attention REPLICATED** | 6.70 GB | 4.61 GB | 0.67 GB | **11.98 GB** | **~224** |
| **EP8, attention TP2-split** | 3.35 GB | 4.61 GB | 0.67 GB | **8.63 GB** | **~311** |
| **Pure TP8 (recommended)** | 0.84 GB | 1.77 GB (8/8, no imbalance) | 0.08 GB | **2.695 GB** | **~994** |

**Verdict and reconciliation:** the honest EP8 weight-only ceiling is **~224-311 tok/s depending on whether attention is replicated or TP2-split** (it is usually replicated or partially replicated in hybrid EP, so ~224-285 is the realistic band). The old "3.82 GB" was a phantom — it matches no consistent ledger. The "~285 tok/s" prose figure happens to land mid-band but was never derived; it should be quoted as **~250 +/- 35 tok/s (EP8 honest weight-only)**. The inversion is REAL and in fact *stronger* than the old number: TP8 (~994 weight-only) vs EP8 (~224-311) is a **3.2-4.4x weight-only gap**, of which §3 keeps the e2e-realizable portion (~1.6-2.0x after the comms/sampling floor applies to both).

**Why the imbalance, not just the byte count, is the load-bearing effect.** Decode TPOT is set by the per-step *max* over ranks, and that max is a per-token random variable (the realized busiest count is 1-8, not always 2.6). The right statistic is the **time-averaged per-step max**, which for 8-balls-8-bins is E[max]=2.60 but with non-trivial variance (P(max>=4)~18%, P(max>=5)~4%); a single stream feels that variance as jitter, and TPOT integrates the max, so the effective multiplier on the *expert term* is ~2.60 with a long right tail. A well-implemented EP path can overlap the all-to-all dispatch with the (nonexistent here) shared-path GEMV — but this model has **no shared expert**, so there is nothing to overlap the dispatch against, removing EP's main latency-hiding trick. This is an architecture-specific aggravator of the EP penalty, not a generic one.

| Config | Per-GPU active bytes | Weight-only TPOT | Weight-only ceiling | Realistic e2e @4K |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TP8 FP8 (recommended) | 2.695 GB | 1.01 ms | **~994 tok/s** | ~500-547 tok/s |
| EP8 FP8 (playbook), busiest-rank | 8.6-12.0 GB | 3.2-4.5 ms | **~224-311 honest** | ~220-285 tok/s |
| TP8 FP4-mixed | ~1.4 GB | 0.52 ms | **~1900 (raw) / ~1500-1700 (haircut)** | ~1000-1500 tok/s |

Where the time goes (TP8 FP8, ctx=4K): weight-read ~70%, comms (8-way all-reduce, latency-bound, §1.10) ~18-22%, sampling/host ~5%, KV ~4%. The comms floor is the irreducible residual and its RELATIVE share grows as you shrink the weight term with quant (toward 30-35% at FP4) — making comms-overlap the binding constraint in the aggressive regime.

### 1.6 The byte ledger arithmetic (so every number above is auditable)

- Attention/layer = Wq(4096x8192) + Wk(4096x512) + Wv(4096x512) + Wo(8192x4096) = 71.30 M params; x94 = **6.702 B**.
- One expert (SwiGLU gate+up+down) = 3 x 4096 x 1536 = 18.87 M; x8 active x94 = **14.194 B**.
- Router = 4096 x 128 x94 = **0.049 B**. lm_head = 4096 x 151936 = **0.622 B**.
- Sum = **21.568 B active**; at FP8 (1 B/param) = **21.57 GB**, /8 GPUs = **2.695 GB/GPU** (TP8).

### 1.7 Sensitivity to the realized-HBM-BW constant (the un-sourced 0.80)

The entire roofline hinges on usable BW = 0.80 x 3.35 = 2.68 TB/s. This derating is an assumption (refresh overhead, partial-burst GEMV access, ECC). **All relative lever rankings move with it**, so here is the sweep:

| Realized fraction | Usable BW | TP8 FP8 weight-only ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| 0.70 (pessimistic, poor coalescing) | 2.345 TB/s | ~870 tok/s |
| **0.80 (baseline)** | 2.68 TB/s | **~994 tok/s** |
| 0.85 (well-coalesced, contiguous KV, split-K) | 2.85 TB/s | ~1057 tok/s |

The +/-10% swing here **swamps several Tier 3-5 levers** (e.g. small-message comms ~1.05x, custom GEMV ~1.10x). Practical consequence: **measure realized BW first** (a single large-tensor streaming GEMV microbenchmark) before trusting any sub-1.1x lever ranking. The 0.85 case is reachable precisely *because* of Tier 0/5 levers (contiguous KV + split-K coalescing), so the BW constant is partly an outcome, not just an input.

### 1.8 FP4-on-Hopper: realized-BW, not free (confidence corrected)

H100 has **no native FP4 tensor path**. The win is purely the halved HBM read; the FP4->FP8 unpack runs in the GEMV epilogue and costs extra register/L2 traffic and instruction slots. At B=1 compute has enormous slack, so the unpack is hidden behind the HBM-bound GEMV — but "captured in full" overstated it. The realistic capture is **~85-92% of the theoretical 2x** (the residual is L2 pressure from scale tensors + unpack instructions occasionally exposing on the smallest GEMVs). So FP4-mixed realistic weight-only ceiling is **~1500-1700 tok/s, not the raw 1900**. This is now consistently **medium-low confidence** here and in §4 (prior drafts marked it low-confidence in the table but stated it declaratively in §4 — fixed).

### 1.9 KV crossover, reconciled with the recommended layout

This is the number prior drafts got wrong. **The recommended layout is weight-TP8.** With nkv=4 < 8, TP8 *cannot* split the 4 KV heads across 8 GPUs — so under naive TP8, **KV is replicated** and each GPU reads the FULL 94 KB/pos (FP8). Solving KV_bytes_per_gpu(ctx) = weight_bytes_per_gpu = 2.695 GB:

- **TP8, KV replicated, FP8:** 94 KB x ctx = 2.695 GB -> crossover **~28.7K**, NOT 64-89K.
- **TP8, KV replicated, INT4:** 48 KB x ctx -> crossover **~56K**.

The only way to push the crossover out is to **decouple the KV layout from the weight layout**: shard KV TP4 (1 KV head per 4 GPUs, the other 4 GPUs mirror), giving 24 KB/pos/GPU FP8 -> crossover **~112K**. This is the layout this document recommends for long-context: **weight-TP8 for the GEMVs, KV-TP4 (replicated in 2 groups) for attention.** The earlier "64-89K" figure implicitly assumed this TP4 split *and* a recent-window FP16 carve-out but never stated either — it is replaced by the explicit pair above (**~29K naive-TP8 / ~112K with TP4-split KV**, FP8). The honest summary: *if you do nothing special, KV co-dominates by ~29K; the TP4-split-KV trick buys you out to ~112K.*

### 1.10 The comms floor, derived (not asserted)

Prior drafts quoted "~0.28ms 8-way all-reduce floor" with no derivation, leaving it ambiguous whether that was per-collective or per-step. Here it is built up:

- **Per step there are 2 all-reduces/layer x 94 layers = 188 collectives** (the attention-output reduction and the expert-output reduction; under TP8 the expert reduction *folds into* the same all-reduce path, but they remain 2 logical reductions per layer in the residual chain).
- The reductions **are serialized by the residual dependency chain** — layer L+1 cannot start its attention before layer L's MoE reduction lands. So you cannot hide them behind each other across layers; you can only shrink each one's fixed latency.
- Payload is ~8 KB (hidden=4096 x 2 B), wire time ~18 ns — **negligible; the cost is entirely fixed launch+sync latency per collective.**
- Per-collective exposed latency x 188 = per-step comms floor:

| Per-collective exposed latency | Per-step comms (x188) |
|---|---|
| 0.75 us (fused one-shot, persistent SMs) | **~0.14 ms** |
| 1.0 us (tuned one-shot LL, 1-2 channels) | ~0.19 ms |
| 1.5 us (stock LL128, multi-channel) | ~0.28 ms |
| 2.0 us (ring, default channels) | ~0.38 ms |

So the often-quoted **~0.28ms/step is the *stock-LL128* operating point** (1.5 us x 188), and Tier 3's small-message comms lever pushes it to ~0.14-0.19 ms by cutting per-collective latency, NOT by overlapping across layers (which the residual chain forbids). This makes the floor auditable and shows exactly which lever moves it. **Overlap assumption stated explicitly: collectives are serialized per-layer; the only freedom is per-collective latency.** The residual ~0.3-0.5 ms "comms+sampling+host" floor = comms (0.14-0.28) + sampling/argmax-sync (~0.05-0.1) + host residual after graphs (~0.05-0.1).

## 2. Ranked Implementation Roadmap (gain-per-effort)

Each lever states what bytes/latency it removes and whether it is independent of or overlaps others. **Do not multiply these — Section 3 gives the honest stacked estimate.** Levers with a quality cost now carry an explicit **eval gate** (benchmark + expected delta + source).

### Tier 0 — Prerequisites (config-level, do first, high confidence)

**1. CUDA graphs + contiguous KV.** Capture the full B=1 decode step (including the sampling tail) once at warmup and replay; drop paged-KV for a single contiguous per-sequence buffer sized to max context, with length as the only varying input. Removes the ~4.2ms/token launch tax (94 layers x ~15 kernels x ~3us) -> ~0, and removes the paged block-table indirection that hurts the coalesced KV reads the flash-decode kernel wants (this also helps push realized BW toward the 0.85 case in §1.7). **Gain ~1.5-2.5x vs ungraphed. Prerequisite for every later lever.** Effort: low.

**2. Serving-layer fast path.** Scheduler-free single-request loop (no queue/admission/batch assembly), on-device sampled-token self-feedback (no per-token device->host->device PCIe round-trip of the sampled id), async detokenize (one token behind the GPU), isolated API-server process (kills vLLM's ~33% GIL-contended SSE slice), on-device EOS/stop checks. Disable chunked prefill and PD-disaggregation (both are throughput tools that only add latency at B=1). **Gain ~1.05-1.15x incremental over graphs.** Effort: medium-high (custom runner), but the config-level pieces ship today.

**3. FP8 weights + FP8/INT8 KV.** The dominant-term halving vs FP16. **~1.5-2x vs FP16; ~1x if already FP8** (the assumed baseline). Eval gate: FP8 (E4M3) weight quant on Qwen3-235B is essentially lossless — expect **<0.3% MMLU / <0.5% on code-eval**, consistent with published FP8 PTQ results on large MoEs (NVIDIA TensorRT-LLM / SGLang FP8 reports). Effort: low (offline quant + flag).

### Tier 1 — The structural re-layout (the big honest jump, medium confidence)

**4. EP8 -> pure TP8.** This is the single most load-bearing change and it CONTRADICTS the playbook. The full busiest-rank ledger is in **§1.5**: EP8's honest weight-only ceiling is **~224-311 tok/s** (attention replicated -> TP2-split), because at B=1 one token's 8 experts scatter as balls-in-bins (E[max busiest]=2.60) and decode waits on the busiest rank, and this model's **lack of a shared expert removes EP's dispatch-overlap hiding trick**. Pure TP8 holds a column slice of *all 128 experts* on every GPU, reads exactly 8/8 of each active expert's columns — **routing imbalance structurally eliminated** — per-GPU weight-read drops to **2.695 GB**, and the expert reduction **folds into the all-reduce already on the attention path** (TP8 = 2 collectives/layer; EP8 = ~4, including all-to-all). TP8 moves fewer bytes AND issues fewer collectives. **Gain ~1.6-2.0x e2e at short ctx (~250 -> ~500-547 tok/s).** Note the weight-only gap is larger (3.2-4.4x) but the shared comms/sampling floor (which both layouts pay) compresses the *realizable* gain. **Gated on the TP8-MoE kernel-efficiency haircut — quantified in §2a below, the load-bearing prerequisite.** Effort: medium (config + validation).

#### §2a. The TP8-MoE kernel-efficiency risk (the prerequisite, now quantified)

The TP8 win is only real if the column-sharded MoE GEMV realizes BW comparably to EP's contiguous-block GEMV. Under TP8, each of 128 experts is column-sharded to 1536/8 = **192 intermediate cols/expert/GPU**, and the kernel must gather the 8 active experts out of 128 and run 8 very skinny (hidden=4096 -> 192) GEMVs per GPU. EP instead runs ~2.6 *contiguous full-width* (4096->1536) expert blocks. The skinny TP8 GEMVs have (a) worse weight-tile reuse in L2, (b) a 1-of-128 scatter/gather that doesn't vectorize as cleanly, and (c) more kernel-launch/epilogue overhead per active expert.

**Break-even analysis.** EP8 busiest-rank ceiling ~250 tok/s (attention-replicated band); TP8 raw ~994. For TP8 to still win, its realized efficiency e must satisfy 994·e > 250, i.e. **e > 0.25** — an enormous margin. Even a brutal column-sharding haircut to **e ≈ 0.60** (vs EP's assumed ~0.80) yields 994 x (0.60/0.80) ≈ **~745 weight-only / ~450-500 e2e**, still comfortably above EP's ~250. **The inversion survives any realistic haircut**; it would only evaporate if TP8-MoE ran below ~0.30 efficiency, which would indicate a broken kernel, not a layout penalty. So the prerequisite is real but the *margin* is huge — this de-risks the recommendation substantially versus leaving it a bare "measure first." The action remains: measure TP8-MoE realized BW, but the decision is robust unless it is catastrophically bad.

### Tier 2 — The orthogonal multiplier (medium confidence)

**5. Speculative decoding (EAGLE-3 / native MTP).** The ONLY lever that beats the per-step weight-read floor: it reduces the number of memory passes per emitted token, stacking multiplicatively on every quant/parallelism win. Use n-gram/PLD as the zero-train first move (tau 1.2-1.6 general, 3-5 on code/JSON), then EAGLE-3 or the Qwen3 MTP head.

**The explicit speedup formula** (prior drafts hand-waved "~1.15-1.3x"):

```
spec_speedup = tau / (1 + c * draft_positions)
```

- **tau** = mean accepted LENGTH (a draft-tree PATH statistic — *not* the per-token acceptance RATE the console shows). This is the number of tokens emitted per verify pass.
- **draft_positions** = total positions verified in the tree (breadth x depth, minus root).
- **c** = the per-draft-position byte-overhead of the verify pass *relative to a single decode step's weight read*. Its derivation: a single decode step reads the 8 active experts (one token's top-8). A verify pass over D draft positions reads the **UNION of experts** touched by all D positions. As D grows, the union approaches the full 128 experts (saturating the 66% expert term at ~14.19 GB / 2.695 GB·layer⁻¹... i.e. up to the full read). For Qwen3 (66-80% routed mass, top-8 of 128), the union grows roughly as 128·(1-(120/128)^(8D)) experts; normalizing the *extra* bytes per draft position against the baseline single-token expert read gives **c ≈ 0.27/draft-position** (~5x a dense model's c≈0.05, because dense models re-read the same FFN regardless of D, so their verify tax is near-zero per position).

**Consequence — bushy trees go net-negative.** Plugging in (verified arithmetic):

| tau | c | draft_positions | speedup |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.74 | 0.27 | 8 (bushy) | **0.70 (LOSS)** |
| 2.0 | 0.27 | 6 | **0.76 (LOSS)** |
| 1.74 | 0.27 | 2 (narrow/shallow) | **1.13** |
| 2.0 | 0.15 (shallow union) | 4 | **1.25** |

This is the whole reason the doc prescribes **NARROW/DEEP** (better: narrow/shallow with few draft positions) trees on this MoE — the dense-model bushy-tree default is *net-negative* here. **tau=1.74-2.2 is attributed to AngelSlim/EAGLE-3 reports on Qwen3 but should be treated as SPECULATIVE for this exact checkpoint** until measured — published EAGLE-3 tau on comparable MoEs falls in ~1.6-2.3 general / higher on code, and the realized wall-clock speedup is the formula above, not tau itself. Realistic e2e: **~1.13-1.25x FP8 short-ctx**, rising to **~1.4-1.6x at 32-64K+** because the verify pass amortizes the exposed KV read once per tree. **Not double-counted against comms:** the verify pass still pays the per-step comms floor once per tree, which is *already inside* the formula's denominator effect (fewer verify passes per token = fewer comms-floor payments) — spec is the one lever that also reduces comms-floor frequency. Effort: medium (pretrained head + tree-shape sweep).

### Tier 3 — Comms and quant frontier (medium-low confidence)

**6. Small-message comms.** Per §1.10 the per-step comms floor is 188 collectives x per-collective fixed latency. At 8KB the wire time is ~18ns; the cost is launch+sync. Use custom one-shot/two-shot all-reduce (flat 2 hops vs ring's 2(N-1)), plain LL (not LL128) and 1-2 channels (not 32) at this size, and a low-latency EP all-to-all if any EP remains. The deeper win fuses the reduction into the GEMV epilogue (persistent comms, ~6-20 SMs), cutting per-collective latency from ~1.5us (stock) toward ~0.75us -> **per-step comms 0.28ms -> ~0.14ms**. **~1.05-1.10x at FP8 short-ctx**; structural value is keeping comms flat as you raise TP, which UNLOCKS affordable TP4 for long-context KV (§1.9). Effort: medium.

**7. FP8 -> FP4-mixed weights.** Quantize by bandwidth-share x sensitivity: routed experts (66%, LEAST sensitive) -> MXFP4/INT4; attention/lm_head -> FP8; router -> BF16 (never quantize — 0.2% of bytes, discrete top-8 selection). On Hopper, FP4 dequants in-kernel to FP8 (no native FP4 path — **§1.8**), capturing ~85-92% of the 2x BW win, not 100%. **~1.13-1.20x e2e short-ctx** for the mixed path. **Eval gate:** routed-expert-only MXFP4/INT4 (group-wise, g=128) is the regime W4A16-style PTQ handles best on large MoEs — expect **<0.5-1.0% MMLU and <1.5% on code/math** (consistent with GPTQ/AWQ-class 4-bit results on 200B+ MoEs); *uniform* FP4 including attention is riskier (**1-3% potential drop**, attention outliers). **Acceptance threshold: ship routed-FP4 only if MMLU-redux delta < 1.0% and HumanEval delta < 2%; otherwise fall back to FP8 attention + FP4 experts.** W4A4 is a throughput trap — zero B=1 benefit, adds activation-quant error; do NOT enable. Effort: medium.

### Tier 4 — Attention and long-context (context-gated)

**8. Split-KV / flash-decoding occupancy.** With 4 KV heads (TP8 forces KV replication, or TP4-split per §1.9), the decode attention grid launches ~2-4 CTAs on 132 SMs -> ~1.5-3% occupancy, so it reads KV at a small fraction of HBM BW (launch-grid-bound). The stock FA3 heuristic ABORTS splitting for low-head-count models like this one. Override the split count to target ~132 CTAs (S~64) + tensor-core GQA head-group fusion (16 Q heads sharing each KV head -> clean 16x128 MMA tile). Separately, a distinct FA3 prefill kernel for TTFT (see §2b). **~1.0x at ctx<=8K, ~1.05-1.2x at 64-128K.** Effort: low.

**9. KV quantization (KIVI-style).** K and V quantized DIFFERENTLY: per-CHANNEL scales for K (fixed outlier channels), per-TOKEN scales for V (free at B=1 — one token appended per step), plus a FP16 sink+recent window. Enables INT4 KV (48 KB/pos). **Crossover for THIS model (reconciled in §1.9): ~29K FP8 / ~56K INT4 under naive TP8-replicated KV; out to ~112K FP8 with TP4-split KV.** **Eval gate:** KIVI-INT4 (asymmetric, K-per-channel/V-per-token, 32-group, FP16 recent window=32) reports **<0.5-1% on long-context retrieval/QA** for large models; **acceptance: RULER/needle-in-haystack accuracy delta < 1% vs FP16 KV at target ctx, else INT8 KV (94->...) fallback.** **Negligible <16K; ~1.33x at 128K quant-only; up to ~1.6x with SnapKV/H2O eviction** (attacks the count C, orthogonal to per-byte quant) on eviction-tolerant tasks — eviction eval gate: **<1-2% on the specific task's recall** (eviction is task-sensitive; do NOT enable for exact-retrieval workloads). MLA cannot be retrofitted — ruled out. Effort: low (FP8) to medium (INT4 kernel).

#### §2b. TTFT / prefill — its own roofline (the under-built half, now built)

The stated objective includes *minimize TTFT*. Prefill is a **different regime from decode**: over a P-token prompt the projection/MoE GEMMs have M=P, so they are **compute-bound (GEMM)** for long prompts and only bandwidth-bound for very short ones. Derivations (active params 21.57B, 8xH100 FP8 dense = 8 x 1979 = 15.8 PF, ~60% MFU realistic):

**(a) Compute-bound projection/FFN term.** FLOPs ≈ 2 x 21.57B x P. TTFT_compute ≈ 2·21.57e9·P / (15.8e15·0.6):

| P (prompt) | Prefill GEMM FLOPs | Compute-bound TTFT (60% MFU) |
|---|---|---|
| 512 | 22 TF | **~2.3 ms** |
| 2048 | 88 TF | **~9.3 ms** |
| 8192 | 353 TF | **~37 ms** |
| 32768 | 1.41 PF | **~149 ms** |

**(b) Bandwidth<->compute crossover.** The weights are read ONCE for the whole prefill chunk (one pass, ~1.0 ms at 21.57 GB / 21.4 TB/s aggregate). Setting compute-time = single-weight-read-time gives crossover **P ≈ 222 tokens**. So: **prompts < ~220 tokens are bandwidth-bound (TTFT floored by the ~1 ms weight read); prompts > ~220 tokens are compute-bound** and TTFT scales linearly with P (until attention quadratics kick in).

**(c) Attention-quadratic term** (2·2·P²·nq·head_dim·layers, both QK and AV):

| P | Attention FLOPs | Attention TTFT |
|---|---|---|
| 2048 | 13 TF | ~1.4 ms |
| 8192 | 207 TF | ~22 ms |
| 32768 | 3.3 PF | **~348 ms** (now DOMINATES the linear FFN term) |
| 131072 | 53 PF | ~5.6 s |

**Crossover where attention overtakes FFN: ~24-32K prompt tokens.** Past that, prefill TTFT is dominated by the O(P²) attention, and FlashAttention-3's compute efficiency (and chunking to keep working sets in SRAM) is what matters — *not* the decode-side bandwidth levers.

**Chunked vs single-shot.** At B=1 with no other requests, **single-shot prefill is optimal up to the point where the attention working set blows L2/SMEM** (roughly P > ~8-16K) — beyond that, **chunked prefill (chunk ~2-4K) wins** by keeping FA3 tiles resident and overlapping the FFN GEMM of chunk i with attention of chunk i+1. So the §2 "disable chunked prefill" advice is **decode-specific and long-prompt-conditional**: disable it for *short* prompts (pure latency overhead), enable it for *long* (>~16K) prompts where it improves attention locality. Prefix caching (reuse KV for shared system prompts) removes the entire prefill of the cached span — the single biggest TTFT lever when prompts share a prefix. Effort: low-medium.

### Tier 5 — Surgery and research-grade kernels (low confidence, last)

**10. Dynamic top-k + static depth pruning.** top-8 -> top-4 hits the dominant 66% term directly; idling 4/8 GPUs is HARMLESS at B=1 and shrinks comms fan-out. Confidence-adaptive k via the fp32 router softmax costs nothing. **Eval gate (the critic's key demand):** "top-4 is greedy-neutral" is NOT free on this model — published MoE top-k reduction shows **top-6 ≈ <0.5% degradation, top-4 ≈ 1-3% on reasoning/code** (the tail experts carry minority-but-nonzero signal; greedy decoding tolerates it better than sampling). **Acceptance: confidence-adaptive (k=4 only when router top-4 mass > 0.9, else k=8) to bound worst-case to <0.5% MMLU; static top-4 only if MMLU delta < 1.5% AND HumanEval delta < 3%.** FFN-only depth skip (keep attention every layer for KV consistency): **literature shows ~10-15% of FFN layers skippable at <1% loss on large models; deeper pruning costs 2-5%** — gate per-task. **~1.10x (adaptive top-6, low risk) to ~1.30x (top-4 + light FFN-skip, quality-gated).** Width/neuron pruning is USELESS at B=1 (GEMV). Effort: low to medium.

**11. Custom split-K dequant GEMV kernels.** At B=1 the GEMV runs as a CUDA-core streaming-reduction (NOT wgmma — padding M to use tensor cores reads identical weights for 8-64x useless FLOPs). Split-K over the contraction dim to fill 132 SMs, 2-stage TMA double-buffer, bit-trick dequant fused into the GEMV (never a separate dequant-to-HBM pass — this is also what keeps the FP4 unpack of §1.8 in-register), DSMEM cluster reduction, L2-pin per-token-constant tensors (router, scales, RMSNorm). This is the lever that realizes the §1.7 0.85 BW case. **~1.10-1.18x vs a tuned baseline.** Effort: high.

**12. Persistent / mega-kernel.** One grid launch for the whole decode step; removes inter-kernel memory-pipeline bubbles and tail effects graphs leave (graphs remove CPU launch cost only; the 50%->78% HBM-utilization jump is bubbles, not launch). Needs FlashMoE-style RUNTIME in-kernel expert routing — Mirage/MPK cannot do MoE (static task graph). Device-initiated NVSHMEM converts the exposed all-reduce/all-to-all into fine-grained overlap — the only path that attacks the §1.10 per-layer serialization itself. **~1.15-1.25x over a graphed+fused stack.** Effort: research-grade.

## 3. Honest Cumulative Estimate vs the Ceiling

**The roadmap multipliers MUST NOT be multiplied.** Most overlap on the same weight-read term (quant, TP layout, GEMV kernels all divide the SAME bytes) and share a fixed ~0.3-0.5ms comms/sampling/host floor (§1.10) that no byte-lever reduces.

Honest accounting at ctx<=8K, FP8:

- **Ceiling:** TP8 FP8 weight-only = 2.695 GB / 2.68 TB/s = **~994 tok/s**. After the per-step comms floor (~0.14-0.28 ms, §1.10) + sampling + residual host: realistic **~500-547 tok/s e2e**.
- **Starting point:** a naive ungraphed FP8 EP8 stack realizes only tens of tok/s (launch tax + EP busiest-rank imbalance + 62%-CPU host overhead).
- **Stacking without double-counting:** Tier 0 (graphs + fast-path + contiguous KV) gets you into the GPU-bound regime. The EP8->TP8 re-layout is the big honest jump (**~250 -> ~500-547 tok/s**, §1.5; weight-only gap is 3.2-4.4x but the shared comms floor compresses realizable gain to ~1.6-2.0x) — it RE-ALLOCATES where bytes live, so it cannot be multiplied by a quant win on the same bytes. Spec-decode is the one clean multiplicative lever (formula §2 Tier 2): **~600-685 tok/s e2e** at ctx<=8K on FP8 (using ~1.13-1.25x).
- **Adding FP4-mixed weights** lowers the weight floor (~994 raw / ~1500-1700 with the §1.8 haircut weight-only ceiling); the SAME spec multiplier rides it -> realistic **~1000-1500 tok/s e2e** (eval-gated per Tier 3 #7).
- **Long context (64-128K)** is gated by the nkv=4 KV wall (§1.9). Under naive TP8-replicated KV the crossover is ~29K and KV co-dominates early; with TP4-split KV + KIVI-INT4 the crossover pushes to ~112K. Achievable rate falls to **~250-450 tok/s** at 64-128K even with KIVI-INT4 + spec.

**Defensible 8xH100 single-stream target: ~600-950 tok/s (FP8) / ~1000-1500 tok/s (FP4-mixed) at short context** — roughly 50-75% of the moving roofline ceiling after the irreducible comms+sampling floor (and itself +/-10% on the §1.7 BW constant). The remaining gap is the per-layer comms serialization (§1.10), closable only by a megakernel with in-kernel NVSHMEM.

## 4. Genuinely Novel / Non-Obvious Ideas (beyond the playbook)

1. **EP is the WRONG axis at B=1 — with the ledger shown.** Busiest-rank EP8 = **~224-311 tok/s** (full byte ledger §1.5: attention 6.70/3.35 GB + E[max]=2.60 x 1.774 GB busiest experts + 0.67 GB heads), and imbalance multiplies **only the 66% expert term** because attention is balanced under any layout. This model's **missing shared expert** also removes EP's dispatch-overlap hiding trick. Pure TP8 (~994 weight-only, ~500-547 e2e) eliminates the imbalance tax AND has fewer collectives (expert reduction folds into the attention all-reduce). Inverts the playbook's #1 recommendation — and the gap is *larger*, not smaller, than prior drafts claimed.

2. **The config kills two phantoms.** No shared expert, no dense layers (all 94 MoE); attention is 6.70B not ~1B (8192 inner dim). You read only the 8 ACTIVE experts (~1.77 GB/GPU under TP8) per token — EP's "/8 on 14.2B" is illusory at B=1; sharding inactive experts saves zero bandwidth, only HBM capacity (which fits).

3. **tau, not rate; the explicit spec formula; and the MoE verify tax.** speedup = tau/(1 + c·draft_positions), c≈0.27/position from expert-UNION byte growth (~5x dense). **Bushy trees go net-negative (0.70x at dp=8)**; only narrow/shallow trees clear 1.0. tau=1.74-2.2 is treated as speculative pending measurement.

4. **Quantize the BIG thing HARDEST.** The 66% routed-expert bandwidth is LEAST sensitive (routed-FP4 eval gate: <1% MMLU); the 0.2% router is MOST sensitive (never quantize). FP4-on-Hopper captures ~85-92% (not 100%) of the BW win via dequant-to-FP8 because compute has slack — the "Blackwell-only" caveat is throughput advice. (Confidence now consistent with §1.8.)

5. **Standard serving machinery is anti-B=1.** Paged KV, continuous-batch scheduler, **chunked prefill (except long-prompt prefill, §2b)**, PD-disaggregation, W4A4, NVLS/SHARP, LL128, max-channels=32 are throughput advice. vLLM's 62%-CPU low-batch profile says: turn this OFF. The MoE all-to-all is 8KB (~18ns) — a pure latency event.

6. **Expert-prefetch literature is moot.** All 235GB FP8 are HBM-resident — no PCIe fetch to hide. The only deterministic prefetch target would be a shared expert, which this model lacks.

7. **SRAM machines invert on MoE.** Sparsity is a gift to HBM machines (read only active) but a curse to Cerebras/Groq (store all 235B), and dynamic routing breaks their static dataflow. Dense single-stream records do not transfer.

8. **Prefill and decode are opposite regimes.** Decode is bandwidth-bound (GEMV, AI~1); prefill is **compute-bound past P~222 tokens** and **attention-quadratic-dominated past ~24-32K** (§2b). The decode-side bandwidth levers do NOT help long-prompt TTFT — FA3 compute efficiency + chunking does. Spec-decode compounds with long context (verify pays comms once per tree), the opposite of intuition.

## 5. The "Different Architecture for Single-User" Thesis

### 5.1 Model architecture

B=1-optimal = minimize active bytes/token AND the per-step dependency chain. Levers: aggressive GQA (4 KV heads here is near-ideal) — but MLA is architectural and cannot be retrofitted (rule out); FEWER but WIDER experts so a token's top-k spreads less (shrinks both the c≈0.27 verify tax and EP imbalance); a NATIVE MTP head shipped with the checkpoint (zero-train spec-decode); shallower/prunable depth (the per-step all-reduce floor scales with the 94-layer count — §1.10 — so depth is doubly costly at B=1). Dynamic top-k (top-8->top-4, eval-gated §Tier5 #10) is a runtime MoE-only knob. A truly B=1-tuned variant co-trains MTP + a depth-prunable schedule + FP4-robust expert weights.

### 5.2 Hardware architecture

B=1 cares only about realized single-step bandwidth + the fixed latency floor. **8xB200: ~2.0-2.5x** (2.39x HBM-BW step diluted by the BW-insensitive comms floor) + native NVFP4 (so the §1.8 Hopper-dequant haircut vanishes — FP4 runs in-tensor-core) + 5th-gen NVLink (1.8 TB/s, halves the §1.10 per-collective latency) + 126MB L2 — lowest-risk lever if procurement allows. **GB200 NVL72 headline numbers are THROUGHPUT** and must not be cited for B=1; honest B=1 min-latency is in the hundreds of tok/s. **SRAM machines** structurally beat the HBM re-read floor but MoE inverts their economics — research bet. Apple M3 Ultra: capacity-bound, ~30-60 tok/s, a cost play not a latency play.

### 5.3 Engine code-path

A B=1 engine should be a SEPARATE code path: scheduler-free single-request loop, contiguous KV (no block manager), on-device sampled-token self-feedback, async detokenize, CUDA-graph capture including the sampling tail, on-device EOS/stop, DISABLED continuous-batching/chunked-prefill(short-prompt)/PD-disagg. The kernel-side endpoint is a persistent megakernel (FlashMoE-style runtime routing + device-initiated NVSHMEM) the host loop must not re-enter per token. SGLang is the closest mainstream stack but **its TP-MoE path must clear the §2a efficiency bar (e > ~0.30, with huge margin) before the TP8 win is trusted** — measure realized TP8-MoE BW first.

## Appendix A — The 15 avenues, verified

Each was independently researched, then an adversarial reviewer tried to refute the speedup against the roofline. *Verified speedup* = what survived.

### Bytes-per-token budget & roofline accounting  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** ~1.5-1.9x (central ~1.6-1.7x) for TP2xEP8 -> TP8 at short context, from eliminating the EP routing-imbalance tax. The honest re-floor of TP2xEP8 itself to ~285 tok/s (vs the frame's optimistic 470-630, which assumed perfect EP balance) is the most defensible and load-bearing result. Combined with spec-decode the avenues OVERLAP (spec-decode fills EP bins, shrinking the tax), so do not naively multiply: realistic stacked ~285 -> ~470-547 (TP8) x ~2.0-2.5 (spec, slightly reduced acceptance benefit on the imbalance term) ~= 950-1300 tok/s short-ctx, not 1370."

### Weight quantization frontier (FP8 -> FP4 -> 2-bit)  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** FP8 -> FP4-mixed (experts 4-bit, attention/lm_head FP8, router BF16): ~1.13-1.20x end-to-end TPOT at ctx<=8K (NOT 1.25-1.6x). The headline was inflated by a ~1.9x undercount of attention bytes (3.55B vs correct 6.70B), which overstated the expert bandwidth share (77% vs correct 66%) and the per-GPU FP8 baseline (2.05 GB vs correct ~5.46 GB). The robust removed-bytes delta (~0.33 ms/token from experts->4-bit) is correct. To actually reach ~1.4-1.5x you must go UNIFORM FP4 including attention (~1.69x weight-only, ~1.5x e2e), which contradicts the researcher's own keep-attention-FP8 guidance and accepts higher-sensitivity attention quant. FP8 -> 2-bit-experts: realistically ~1.15-1.25x e2e after LUT decode overhead, not 1.3-1.5x. All multipliers compress toward ~1.1x at ctx>=64K.

### KV cache: quantization, layout, GQA/MLA, long-context regime  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** Context-gated, defensible: <16K ctx -> negligible (<7% TPOT, don't bother) — correctly stated. 32K -> ~11-12% TPOT reduction from KIVI-INT4 (claim said ~13%, slightly high once INT4 scale-metadata overhead is counted). 64K -> ~18-19% (claim ~21%, marginally optimistic). 128K -> ~1.33-1.37x from KIVI-INT4 alone (claim 1.4x; trimmed for ~4.5-bit effective INT4 + FP16 sink window). Eviction stacking to ~1.6-1.7x is real ONLY on eviction-tolerant tasks and ONLY if INT4 accuracy holds on Qwen3-235B (both unvalidated) — so the defensible HEADLINE long-context number is ~1.33x (quant-only, accuracy-gated), with up to ~1.6x as a conditional best case on summarization-style workloads. Net: a genuine but narrow long-context-only lever; zero value in the dominant short-ctx B=1 regime.

### Speculative decoding design space (THE central B=1 lever)  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-now` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** FP8 baseline (CUDA graphs + comms-overlap already applied): ~1.15-1.30× at short ctx (≤8K), rising to ~1.4-1.7× at ctx 32-64K+ as exposed KV read amortizes across the tree. On FP8 TP2×EP8 ~470 tok/s → ~540-610 tok/s short ctx; long-ctx multiplier applies to the (lower) long-ctx baseline. The headline 1.8-2.0× is a real BF16/SGLang measurement but does NOT transfer to an FP8/FP4 baseline (smaller amortizable weight base, fixed expert-union tax c≈0.27). FP4+EAGLE-3 @4K: defensible ~1450-1600 tok/s (NOT 1700-2500), and gated on unvalidated FP4-MoE accuracy.

### MoE-specific B=1: all-to-all, expert prefetch, hot-expert residency  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** ~1.15-1.35x on TPOT at ctx<=8K (FP8), down from the claimed 1.25-1.6x. De-overlapped exposed-ms budget on a ~1.6 ms floor: skew-removal-via-TP ~0.2-0.3 ms (ONE lever, not two), grouped-GEMV occupancy ~0.2-0.3 ms (overlaps skew + recovers the floor rather than stacking under it), shared-expert overlap ~0.1-0.2 ms, megakernel residual beyond CUDA graphs ~0.1-0.2 ms (overlaps the all-to-all term). Realistic exposed saving ~0.3-0.5 ms on 1.6 ms => ~1.2-1.35x. The 1.6x upper bound is rejected: it double-books skew removal (#2 + #7) and stacks occupancy onto a floor that already assumes peak BW.

### Parallelism layout for LATENCY (TP vs EP vs replication)  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** Weight-read term: ~2.5-3.0x (raw 3.67x, discounted to ~2.6x once 8-way all-reduce latency is priced honestly). End-to-end TPOT @4K: ~2.0-2.8x raw, settling to ~1.4-2.0x after a realistic shared fixed-latency floor (lm_head + sampling + un-overlapped launch residual) — the reviewer's stated range is DEFENSIBLE and slightly conservative. @64K: ~1.3-1.6x as KV (capped at 4-way split, 4 KV heads) co-dominates both layouts equally. Net: pure TP8 genuinely beats playbook TP2xEP8 at B=1; the central physics (6.8B TP-shardable non-expert mass + negligible 8-active-expert bytes) is correct and verified against the config."

### Kernel-launch elimination: CUDA graphs vs persistent megakernel  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** ~1.15-1.25x on TPOT over an ALREADY-CUDA-graphed+fused stack at ctx<=8K (~0.25-0.4 ms/token removed; ~426->~490-530 tok/s). Hard ceiling = the 1.586ms FP8 weight-read floor; the megakernel converges effective HBM util toward ~70-78% but does not beat the floor. The ~2.5x vs vLLM headline is REJECTED as the avenue's number because most of it is the CUDA-graphs delta the playbook already counts (C.1). Win shrinks toward 1.0x as ctx grows past ~48-64K (KV-read dominates, unaffected) and is largest under FP4+short-ctx where overhead is 25-30% of a shrunken TPOT."

### GEMV/fusion kernels & H100 microarchitecture exploitation  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** ~1.15-1.25x on TPOT vs a naively-tiled CUDA-graphed FP8 baseline at ctx<=8K (where the GEMV term dominates). The real engine is the BW-utilization recovery on skinny GEMVs (~1.10-1.20x against an untuned baseline, only ~1.05-1.12x against a competent vendor GEMV) plus a genuine but smaller ~0.1-0.3 ms residual-gap removal from megakernel sync/activation-round-trip elimination. The L2-pinning of the shared expert is physically impossible (50 MB L2 vs 300-500 MB), so subtract its claimed 4-7%; only the small router/scale/norm tensors pin (<1%). The ~0.3-0.4 ms/token inter-GPU comms floor caps the achievable end-to-end multiplier regardless. Against a tuned baseline, expect closer to ~1.10-1.18x. Value shrinks at ctx>=48-64K as KV-read co-dominates and the win shifts to attention/KV kernels."

### Collective-comms latency floor (small-message NVLink)  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-now` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** ~1.05-1.10x on TPOT in the FP8 TP2xEP8 regime at ctx=4K (saving ~0.08-0.15 ms exposed, not the claimed 0.15-0.18 ms, because part of the 0.33 ms is already overlapped with the idle-SM GEMV and the launch component is already taken by CUDA graphs). In the FP4 regime the SAME physical saving re-bases to ~1.08-1.14x (not a fresh multiplier on top of FP8). The headline 1.25x (FP8) and 1.35x (FP4) upper bounds are not derivable from the claim's own arithmetic and should be dropped. The genuinely high-value, defensible contribution is structural, not a standalone multiplier: one-shot/two-shot + LL all-to-all is what keeps comms flat (~2 hops) instead of ring's 2(N-1) as you raise TP, which is the enabling condition for TP4 long-context KV splitting and prevents naive-TP regressions."

### Single-query attention decode kernels & long-context  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-now` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** Mechanism is real and correctly roofline-grounded, but the magnitude is baseline-sensitive and the headline numbers are optimistic. Defensible END-TO-END TPOT: ~1.0x at ctx<=8K (attention <5-15% of bytes; negligible), ~1.05-1.15x at 64K and ~1.1-1.2x at 128K WHEN the baseline already uses FlashInfer/Flash-Decoding split-KV (which the playbook and any modern SGLang/vLLM stack do) and you are merely tuning the split count for the 2-KV-head case. The larger 1.3x-1.9x figures only materialize against a naive/generic non-split kernel the target system would not ship -- so treat them as headroom, not deliverable. TTFT: ~1.05-1.2x from a separately-tuned FA3 prefill path vs a generic kernel (less if FA3 prefill is already in use). The '5-50x microkernel' and '3x head-group fusion' are isolated-kernel / batched numbers and are NOT end-to-end gains at B=1.

### Alternative decoding algorithms (parallel/iterative)  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** As a TRAIN-FREE DROP-IN replacing the playbook's n-gram drafter: ~1.5–1.9× on code/structured/repetitive prompts, ~1.2–1.4× on prose — but this REPLACES n-gram (~1.2–1.4×), so the marginal gain over the existing train-free baseline is only ~1.2–1.5×, not 1.5–2× net. As a NET-NEW speedup over the playbook's recommended EAGLE-3/MTP path: ~1.0× (none) — these methods are mutually-exclusive drafters and a well-trained head (~2–3×) dominates. Defensible headline: ~1.5× standalone vs no-spec, but effectively 0 additional over a stack that already plans an EAGLE/MTP head. Jacobi Forcing's 3.0–3.8× is real only after fine-tuning Qwen3-235B and competes with (does not add to) the trained-head budget; realizable on this MoE likely ~2–2.5× after the verify tax and EP all-to-all width cap.

### Model-surgery for latency: layer-skip, early-exit, depth/width pruning  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** Weight-only (as claimed, correct arithmetic): top-6 1.20x, top-4 1.49x, static 15% depth-prune ~1.17x, top-4+15%-prune ~1.74x. END-TO-END single-stream tok/s (the metric that matters, including the ~0.3-0.4ms exposed comms+launch+sampling floor at ctx=4K): top-6 ~1.14x, top-4 ~1.30-1.40x, top-4+15%-prune ~1.45-1.50x. DEFENSIBLE GREEDY-NEUTRAL SHIP: top-6 (~1.14x e2e, low quality risk) or top-4 GATED on per-task eval (~1.35x e2e, quality gamble on a fine-grained MoE). The headline 1.5-1.7x is achievable only as weight-only-best-case combining two unvalidated accuracy bets; discount to ~1.4-1.5x end-to-end with quality caveats.

### Hardware frontier for single-user tok/s  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** Defensible HARDWARE-ONLY: 8xB200 FP8 vs 8xH100 FP8 = ~2.0-2.5x TPOT single-stream (2.39x nameplate BW ratio diluted by the BW-insensitive comms+sampling+launch floor, which grows as a TPOT fraction as the weight term shrinks). Adding native NVFP4 weights (accuracy confirmed on Qwen3-235B-A22B): a further ~1.6-1.9x IF AND ONLY IF that byte-halving is not also credited to a separate quantization avenue. Net silicon+precision: ~3.5-4.5x vs an H100 FP8 baseline -- but the 'B200+NVFP4' portion largely IS the FP4 quant lever in better hardware, so against an H100+FP4 baseline the B200 win collapses back toward ~2.0-2.5x. MTP/EAGLE (~2-3x accepted tokens/pass) is genuinely orthogonal and stacks on any silicon, but note R1's headline includes lossy relaxed acceptance. Cerebras/Groq: NOT defensible for this MoE -- demonstrated only on dense models; 235B MoE needs ~6 wafers and breaks static scheduling, so 3-10x is speculative/unrealized. Bottom line defensible end-to-end: ~2.0-2.5x from silicon alone, ~3.5-4.5x silicon+NVFP4 (non-double-counted), spec multiplies further but is a shared orthogonal lever.

### TTFT / prefill latency for a single request  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-now` · **Verdict:** `mostly-solid`  
**Verified speedup:** Prefix caching on turns 2+: ~5-10x TTFT reduction, defensible but workload-conditional (zero on turn 1 / fresh prompts). FP8 vs FP16 baseline: ~2x TTFT in whichever regime the prompt sits (bandwidth at short P, compute at long P) - but vs the existing FP8 playbook baseline the incremental gain is ~1x (already paid). Single-shot prefill: recover ~10-25% of self-inflicted chunking overhead. Handoff fixes: remove a one-time ~1-5ms hitch. Net DEFENSIBLE incremental TTFT win over the current FP8+CUDA-graph baseline: prefix caching is the only large independent lever (5-10x, conditional on chat reuse); the rest is single-digit-% to ~25% overhead recovery plus a one-time ms hitch. Do NOT multiply the short-P 2x by the long-P 2x.

### Serving-layer & CPU-side overheads (the underrated tax)  
**Recommendation:** `adopt-with-care` · **Verdict:** `shaky`  
**Verified speedup:** ~1.05-1.15× at ctx=4K (≈0.1-0.2 ms/token removed) ASSUMING CUDA graphs and contiguous-KV are already in place — i.e. counting only the graph-resistant residual (scheduler fast-path + on-device sampling feedback + eliminated host syncs). The mechanisms are real and worth implementing, but the 1.3-1.8× headline is inflated by (a) porting a Llama-8B CPU/GPU *ratio* instead of the fixed absolute host cost, and (b) re-billing host overhead the CUDA-graphs avenue already claims. Shrinks toward ~1.02-1.05× at ctx=64K and under spec-decode. If the precondition is RELAXED (no CUDA graphs in the baseline), a larger number appears — but that win belongs to the CUDA-graphs line item, not here.
