Two MBUs, both true: the K5 expert kernel realizes 46% of HBM in isolation (the compute floor is nearly met), but the effective whole-step MBU is far lower — the gap is the launch + all-reduce overhead that inflates wall-time. Closing it (monokernel + NVLS in-switch all-reduce) is the lever, not more kernel tuning.

Decode MFU is ~0.02% — and that is correct: B=1 decode is a memory-bound GEMV stream (one pass over the weights, no reuse), so the tensor cores sit idle and the wall is HBM, not FLOPs. MFU only becomes the right currency in prefill, which is compute-bound. TTFT is modeled (prefill = ctx × 2·active-params ÷ (8 × 1980 TF/s fp8 × 50% assumed MFU)); a measured prefill kernel is the next bench to land it.
