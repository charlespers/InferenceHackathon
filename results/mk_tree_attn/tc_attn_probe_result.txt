CORRECTION (honesty): I attributed the 1.0x->1.3x residual to the "HBM round-trip" of materializing
scores. The arithmetic refutes that as the MAIN cause: scores @M=8/ctx4096 = 64*8*4096*2B = 4MB, round-
trip (GEMM1 write + softmax r/w + GEMM2 read) ~16MB/iter @3350GB/s = ~5us — small vs the +20us residual.
The bulk of the residual is the SEPARATE NAIVE SOFTMAX KERNEL: uncoalesced stride-M reads (S is [M x ctx]
col-major, ldc=M -> consecutive t are M apart) + 3 passes, scaling with the M warp count. A FUSED FA kernel
computes softmax INLINE during the GEMM accumulation (no separate pass, no stride-M spill) -> removes this
residual and should approach the GEMM-only ~1.0x. Net conclusion unchanged (FUSE it), mechanism corrected.

--- DESIGN CONCLUSION (buildable decomposition of the spec-verify attention) ---
The M=k verify attention splits cleanly into two terms:
  (A) SHARED-CONTEXT attn: all M draft queries attend the committed KV [0,ctx). This is the DOMINANT cost
      (ctx large) and is exactly the TC GEMM measured here -> FLAT in M (1.0x), no mask needed.
  (B) DRAFT-SELF attn: each draft position attends the <=k preceding draft tokens in its chain/tree. This is
      a tiny M x M (k<=16) masked attention -> negligible cost; carries the tree/causal mask + per-node RoPE.
=> The M-tax disappears because (A) is TC-flat and (B) is O(M^2)-tiny. The engine verify = cuBLAS/mma TC for
   (A) + a small masked kernel for (B) (my mk_tree_attn handles the masked/tree part). Does NOT require a
   fully-fused monolithic FA kernel to remove the tax. Open Q to Charles: does his verify already separate
   context vs draft-self, and does his engine call cuBLASLt/FA (TC) for the context attn or the warp k2?
